About me

My name is Joshua Christopher Dyson Sykes. I come from a mostly British background, with my whole father’s side being English, and on my mother’s side her mother is English, and father was Italian. I my self are English, born and lived there for 3 years until I migrated here to Australia. Culture wise, I can say that I am fully English, however having an Australian citizenship classifies my partly Australian, though I love to keep to my English roots. I finished year 12 last year and currently in my first year here at RMIT and loving it. I can only speak English, but next semester starting to learn Spanish. I love to travel and with the help of speaking another language will assist me in finding jobs overseas in new and exciting countries.

My interest in IT

My main interest in IT started around the time I got my first DS. That was the first experience of using an IT based system. And from then on, I got addicted to everything thing about IT. I gradually got interested in PC’s in primary school where we had a computer lab full of desktops. I recall messing around with them trying to bypass the sites blocked (Games website), so that me and my friends could play them during computer lap sessions. Ever since then, I have helped around the house with anything needing fixing or helping which involves IT. TV’s, laptops, phones and WIFI. Anything that needed fixing I fixed.

My reasons for coming to RMIT was mainly based on the campus and open day. When I first pondered about going to university in general I first had to decide what course I wanted to do. From the get go, I was already interested in IT and telecommunications, so I decided to choose a course which best suited those needs. I was contemplating between RMIT and Swinburne. However, RMIT has a course (Bachelor of Technology – Computing Studies), which provided the IT interests that I had and the flexibility and a wider selection of elective studies. I also had some recommendations from friends and family saying that it was a good choice.

The main outcomes of what I want to gain from my course, would be the ability to apply my knowledge in telecommunication style jobs. I would expect to come out with the foundation and knowledge which would allow me to further my career in the telecommunications industry and finding a long-term jobs which would provide me with the stability for the long run.
